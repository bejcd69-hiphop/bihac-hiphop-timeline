History Of Hip Hop Culture in Bihać
Formation Period 80s and 90s

📀 Late 1970s – Early 1980s: Foundations of Hip Hop in Bihać

  • Late 1970s: Beytz D began listening to Break Beat and Funk (Sugar Hill Records, Enjoy Records) before the term “Hip Hop” was widely used.
  • Early 1980s: Jasko Pecenkovic "Peceni" (Jasko Pečenković "Pečeni") became Bihać's first B-Boy; formed Brejkeri sa Nove Četvrti (New Quart Breakers) with Beytz D.
  • 1982–1990: New Quart Breakers perform at city schools (Avnoj, USAOJ, Technical High School) and weekend discos Borici (Borići) dorm in New Quart and Brklja, Zegar (Žegar) and Sokolac neighborhoods. Rivals include Harmani B-Boys or Harmani Breakers coming from Harmani/Centre local communities and neighborhoods.
  • Notable city B-Boys: Brki, Pico (Pićo) Copy, Hary, Jakanni (RIP), Dejo, Grba, MCA, Mera MC, MC Burzoti, Pečeni, Dicka (Dićka), Dule, Bestak (Beštak), Dino, Drugi Dino, Boban, Pipi etc.
  • Where music was bought: Beograd shopping mall, RIZ record and cassette music store.

🎧 Radio & Tape Culture of The 80's

  • 1984: Beytz D discovered Rap Attack (Radio Velika Gorica, Slavin Balen); tapes were shared across Bihać.
  • 1986: Beytz D found Electrofunk Premiere on Radio 101; encouraged others to listen Mondays for new Hip Hop music and updates.
  • Significant and influential radio shows of that period such as Po vašem izboru (At Your Request) aired on the First program of Radio Zagreb and Rock Express aired on the Second program of Radio Zagreb hosted by Ante Batinovic (Ante Batinović)

🎬 1984–1986: Hip Hop Cinema Hit Bihać

  • City cinemas screen films like Wild Style, Beat Street, Breakin, Breakin’ 2.
  • These films inspire breakdancing, DJ techniques, Hip Hop fashion, and aerosol art (graffiti) in Bihać.

👥 1985–1986: Rise of the Electroshock Crew

  • The Electroshock Crew formed: DJ Vuki, MC Fresh Mix, Mister B, MCA.
  • After Beytz D joins, crew renames to the Screw Boys.
  • The group grew into a real movement that gathered and united all lovers and devotees of the Hip Hop culture in the city.

🏙️ 1987–1989: Expansion of the Scene

  • 1987: Beytz D moved to Zagreb for university, connected with the Zagreb Hip Hop scene and brought tapes/records back to Bihać.
  • "Rupa" (The Hole) became the key underground club, located in the basement of the Bihać city municipality building.
  • Rupa hosted battles, graffiti art, fashion shows and DJ/MC performances from 1987–1989.

🎤 1989: First Major Hip Hop Event in Bihać

  • First large Hip Hop party held at the Club Azra, Hotel Park organized by the Screw Boys.
  • Hundreds attended; the Screw Boys ran the entire event (flyers, DJs, music).

🎶 The Screw Boys — Music and Breakup

  • The Screw Boys recorded several tracks but none were officially released.
  • Inspired by the movie Colors, they wrote a final hit “NIGHT KILLER”, with major input from Dejan Pavlović and Polar ICE.
  • 1992–1995: The Yugoslav War ends the crew’s journey and dismantles the scene.
