History Of Hip Hop Culture in Bihać
Formation Period 80's and 90's

📀 Late 1970s – Early 1980s: Foundations of Hip Hop in Bihać

  • Late 1970s: Beytz D begins listening to Break Beat and Funk (Sugar Hill Records, Enjoy Records) before the term “Hip Hop” was widely used.
  • Early 1980s: Jasko Pecenkovic (Peceni) (Jasko Pečenković "Pečeni") becomes Bihać's first B-Boy; forms Brejkeri sa Nove Četvrti (New Quart Breakers) with Beytz D.
  • 1982–1990: New Quart Breakers perform at local schools (Avnoj, USAOJ, Technical High School) and weekend discos Brklja, Borici dorm, Zegar (Žegar) and Sokolac. Rivals include Harmani B-Boys or Harmani Breakers.
  • Notable B-Boys: Brki, Pico Copy, Hary, Jakanni (RIP), Dejo, Grba, MCA, Mera MC, MC Burzoti, Pečeni, Dicka, Dule, Bestak, Dino, Drugi Dino, Boban, Pipi, etc.
  • Where music was bought: Beograd shopping mall, RIZ record and cassette music store.

🎧 Radio & Tape Culture of The 80's

  • 1984: Beytz D discovers Rap Attack (Radio Velika Gorica, Slavin Balen); tapes are shared across Bihać.
  • 1986: Beytz D finds Electrofunk Premiere on Radio 101; encourages others to listen Mondays for new Hip Hop music and updates.
  • Significant and influential radio shows of that period such as Po vašem izboru (At Your Request) on the First program of Radio Zagreb and Rock Express on the Second program of Radio Zagreb hosted by Ante Batinovic (Ante Batinović)

🎬 1984–1986: Hip Hop Cinema Hits Bihać

  • City cinemas screen films like Wild Style, Beat Street, Breakin, Breakin’ 2.
  • These films inspire breakdancing, DJ techniques, Hip Hop fashion, and aerosol art (graffiti) in Bihać.

👥 1985–1986: Rise of the Electroshock Crew

  • Electroshock Crew forms: DJ Vuki, MC Fresh Mix, Mister B, MCA.
  • After Beytz D joins, crew renames to Screw Boys.

🏙️ 1987–1989: Expansion of the Scene

  • 1987: Beytz D moves to Zagreb for university, connects with the Zagreb Hip Hop scene, and brings tapes/records back to Bihać.
  • "Rupa" (The Hole) becomes the key underground club, located in the basement of the Bihać city municipality building.
  • Rupa hosts battles, graffiti art, fashion shows, and DJ/MC performances from 1987–1989.

🎤 1989: First Major Hip Hop Event in Bihać

  • First large Hip Hop party held at Club Azra, Hotel Park, organized by Screw Boys.
  • Hundreds attend; Screw Boys run the entire event (flyers, DJs, music).

🎶 Screw Boys — Music and Breakup

  • Screw Boys record several tracks but none are officially released.
  • Inspired by the movie Colors, they write a final hit: “NIGHT KILLER”, with major input from Dejan Pavlović and Polar ICE.
  • 1992–1995: The Yugoslav War ends the crew’s journey and dismantles the scene.
